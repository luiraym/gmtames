How to calculate the AD

Once training and test set have been loaded, just select "calculate->Applicability Domain" In order to calculate the AD methods. The following form for setting the AD options will appear. In the setting form it is possible to check the AD methods to be calculated. Then, just press the "calculate AD" button. Calculation will start and a resume of the status of the calcuations and optimisation procedures (if any) will appear on the MATLAB command window.

Options for AD methods

Data scaling: is the data scaling procedure applied to the dataset for AD methods which are scaling-dependent, such as distance based methods and bounding box based on PCA. Available scaling are: autoscaling, range scaling between 0 and 1, mean centering.

Distance measure: is the distance measure used for distance based methods (distance from centroid and both variable and fixed kNN). Available distance measures are: euclidean, manhattan, mahalanobis.

Distance from centroid - percentile: is the percetile to be used to define the threshold for distance to centroid approach (suggested: 95). Details on how percentile is used to calculate the distance threshold are given in the following paper: F. Sahigara, K. Mansouri, D. Ballabio, A. Mauri, V. Consonni, R. Todeschini, Comparison of different approaches to define the Applicability Domain of QSAR models, Molecules (2012), 17, 4791-4810.

kNN with fixed k - k value: is the k value to be used for the k Nearest Neighbours (kNN) approach with fixed k. Average distances of all the training samples from their k nearest neighbours are calculated and used to define a unique threshold to decide if a test sample is inside or outside the model’s AD. Details are given in the following paper: F. Sahigara, D. Ballabio, R. Todeschini, V. Consonni, Defining a novel k-Nearest Neighbours approach to assess the applicability domain of a QSAR model for reliable predictions, Journal of Cheminformatics (2013), 5:27, 1-9.

kNN with variable k - optimal k value: is the optimal k value for the k Nearest Neighbours (kNN) approach with variable k. The k value can be defined in the associated combo box; moreover, it is possible to set an automatic (or user) selection of the best k value on the basis of a montecarlo procedure. Details are given in the following paper: F. Sahigara, D. Ballabio, R. Todeschini, V. Consonni, Defining a novel k-Nearest Neighbours approach to assess the applicability domain of a QSAR model for reliable predictions, Journal of Cheminformatics (2013), 5:27, 1-9.

Potential functions - kernel: is the kernel of Potential Functions (gaussian or triangular). Details are given in the following papers: T.I. Netzeva, A. Worth, T. Aldenberg, R. Benigni, M.T.D. Cronin, P. Gramatica, J.S. Jaworska, S. Kahn, G. Klopman, C.A. Marchant, et al. Current status of methods for defining the applicability domain of (quantitative) structure-activity relationships. The report and recommendations of ECVAM Workshop 52. ATLA: Alternatives to Laboratory Animals (2005), 33, 155–173. J. Jaworska, N. Nikolova-Jeliazkova, T. Aldenberg. QSAR applicabilty domain estimation by projection of the training set descriptor space: A review. ATLA: Alternatives to Laboratory Animals (2005), 33, 445–459.

Advanced options for AD methods

By clicking on "show advanced settings" it is possible to open enlarge the setting form and change other options for calculating the AD methods.

Leverage - threshold factor: is the factor to be multiplied with the leverage average in order to define the leverage threshold (suggested: 2.5).

kNN with variable k - % validation set, iterations and maximum k value: these are a) percentage of samples to be used in the validation set, b) number of iterations and c) maximum number of neighbours considered during the k-optimization based on the montecarlo procedure for the k Nearest Neighbours (kNN) approach with variable k. Details on the k optimisation are given in the following paper: F. Sahigara, D. Ballabio, R. Todeschini, V. Consonni, Defining a novel k-Nearest Neighbours approach to assess the applicability domain of a QSAR model for reliable predictions, Journal of Cheminformatics (2013), 5:27, 1-9.

Potential functions - smoothing value: is the smoothing coefficient to be used for Potential Functions. Smoothing value shlould be similar to variable standard deviations. The coefficient can be user defined or automatically optimised in leave-one-out.
